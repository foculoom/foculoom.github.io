Current direction

Designed for the first real customer problem.

The current public story is intentionally narrower than the company mission: it leads with the flagship app and the adults it is built to help first.

What the first launch is optimizing for

  • Adults with desk-based work who plan on Mac and execute on iPhone.
  • A calm single-user planner, not a collaborative workspace.
  • A generous free core with an optional Focus & Tasks Plus upgrade for heavier use.
  • Public messaging that stays accurate while the product is still evolving.

What the flagship will keep avoiding

  • Exact launch pricing before it is locked.
  • Medical, therapy, or wellness claims.
  • AI-assistant or automatic-planning promises.
  • Team collaboration, classroom, or caregiver-dashboard positioning.
What is public now

Only Focus & Tasks gets the full product story right now. The next two products stay visible, but clearly secondary.

Principles

How Foculoom is approaching launch

Calm by default

Interfaces should feel focused, clear, and low-friction rather than crowded or high-stimulation.

Accessible and readable

Accessibility is part of the direction from the start, including readable structure, strong contrast, and reduced-motion-friendly choices.

Transparent about what is still changing

This site only publishes what the current business documents can already support. Anything else waits.
